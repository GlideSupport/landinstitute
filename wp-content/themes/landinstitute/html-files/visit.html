The Legacy Beneath Our Feet

Here is the Smoky Hill River watershed and rocks ranging from the Wellington Formation to the Dakota Formation.

Here is a grassland ecosystem, where diverse mixtures of perennial grasses and forbs co-evolved with humans and other animals through fire and grazing.

Here is land usurped from the Kaw, Pawnee, Osage, and other Indigenous peoples, whose ancestors farmed in the river bottomlands, who still exist and persist in dynamic relationships to their homelands, homelands which were also home to hundreds of thousands of bison, countless other mammals, reptiles, birds, invertebrates, all fed by the prairies that helped hold and build soils.

Here is grain agriculture and farming communities, including the city of Salina, Kansas, as a regional trading center, with Euro-American colonization and industrialization featuring widespread tillage into annual agricultural systems.

Here is The Land Institute, founded in 1976. Here we as agricultural researchers benefit from access to land, knowledge, and resources gained due to settler colonialism and other ongoing systems of oppression. Here we commit to responsibly repairing harm and building mutually beneficial relationships with members of the land community across the ecosphere.

Experience The Land Institute

Explore the heart of our prairie research community through guided tours, nature walks, educational programs, and local adventures. There's something inspiring waiting for every visitor.

Weekly Scientist-Led Tours
Join expert-led tours every Friday at 10:00 AM (March 7 – November 7) to explore research plots, campus facilities, and our perennial grain agriculture innovations.
Earth Week Community Events
Special events including Community Tours and a Spring Plant Identification Walk during Earth Week (April 25), offering deeper engagement with our prairie landscape and research.
Field Trips for Schools
Educational field trips available from September 18 – October 31, providing students a hands-on learning experience about sustainable agriculture and ecological research.
Explore the Marty Bender Nature Area
Visit a beautiful 206-acre nature area featuring a 3-mile trail through prairie and woods, scientific research plots, art installations, picnic areas, and scenic overlooks along the Smoky Hill River.
Free Admission and Group Accommodations
Tours are free to attend with advance registration. We also offer customized experiences for larger groups with the support of our tour coordinator.
Salina, Kansas – A Welcoming Community
Discover the vibrant community of Salina, KS, a hub for culture and history, just minutes away from the Land Institute’s campus.
